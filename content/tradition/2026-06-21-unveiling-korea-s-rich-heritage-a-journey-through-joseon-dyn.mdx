Korea's traditional culture is a treasure trove of history, philosophy, and art. At the heart of this heritage lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled the country from 1392 to 1910. During this period, the Korean people developed a unique and distinct culture that continues to influence the country's identity today.

One of the most iconic symbols of Korean traditional culture is the **hanbok**, a beautifully crafted traditional garment that is still worn on special occasions such as weddings and holidays. The hanbok is characterized by its vibrant colors, intricate designs, and elegant silhouette. Visitors to Korea can try on a hanbok and experience the traditional Korean dress for themselves at places like the **Gyeongbokgung Palace** in Seoul.

The royal palaces of Korea are another window into the country's rich cultural heritage. The **Changdeokgung Palace**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Korean history and architecture. The palace's beautiful gardens and traditional buildings offer a glimpse into the lives of the Joseon royalty and the courtiers who served them.

Ancestral rites are an important part of Korean traditional culture, and are still practiced today. The **Jongmyo Shrine**, a sacred place where the spirits of the Joseon kings and queens are honored, is a place of great historical and cultural significance. Visitors can attend a traditional ancestral ritual ceremony, known as **Jongmyo Jerye**, which takes place several times a year.

For those interested in learning more about Korean traditional culture, there are many resources available. The **Korean Folk Village** in Yongin is a great place to start, with its traditional villages, museums, and cultural performances. The **National Museum of Korea** in Seoul is another excellent resource, with its vast collection of artifacts and exhibits on Korean history and culture.

To experience the best of Korean traditional culture, visitors can try the following:
  * Watch a performance of **Noraebang**, a traditional Korean music and dance show
  * Try some **Korean tea**, such as Omija tea or Yuja tea, at a traditional tea house
  * Take a stroll through the **Bukchon Hanok Village**, a traditional Korean village filled with hanok (traditional Korean houses)
