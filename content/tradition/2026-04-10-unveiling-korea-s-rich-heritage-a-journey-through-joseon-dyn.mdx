Korean traditional culture is a treasure trove of vibrant customs, stunning architecture, and profound philosophies. At the heart of this rich heritage lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled Korea from 1392 to 1910. During this period, the kingdom flourished, and its culture evolved into a unique blend of Confucianism, Buddhism, and Shamanism.

One of the most iconic symbols of Korean traditional culture is the **hanbok**, a beautifully crafted traditional garment worn on special occasions. The hanbok is characterized by its vibrant colors, intricate designs, and elegant silhouettes. Visitors can experience the beauty of hanbok by attending a traditional Korean festival or by renting one at a cultural center.

Korea's **royal palaces** are another testament to the country's rich history. The Gyeongbokgung Palace, built in 1395, is the largest and most iconic of the five palaces, showcasing the grandeur of the Joseon dynasty. The Changdeokgung Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is another must-visit destination, featuring a stunning blend of traditional Korean architecture and beautiful gardens.

Ancestral rites, known as **jesa**, play a significant role in Korean traditional culture. These rituals are performed to honor ancestors and pay respects to their memory. Visitors can learn about jesa by attending a traditional Korean ceremony or by visiting the **National Museum of Korea**, which features exhibits on ancestral rites and other aspects of Korean culture.

For those interested in exploring Korean traditional culture, there are several resources available. The **Korean Cultural Heritage Administration** offers guided tours of royal palaces and cultural heritage sites. The **Seoul Metropolitan Government** also provides a range of cultural programs, including traditional Korean music and dance performances.

Recommended resources for further learning include:
* 'Korean History in Maps' by the Korean Cultural Heritage Administration
* 'The Koreans: Who They Are, What They Want, Where Their Future Lies' by Michael Breen
* The **Korea Society**, a non-profit organization promoting Korean culture and education
