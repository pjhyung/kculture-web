Korea's traditional culture is a treasure trove of history, customs, and philosophy. The Joseon dynasty, which lasted from 1392 to 1910, was a pivotal period in Korean history that shaped the country's identity. During this era, Confucianism became the dominant ideology, influencing every aspect of Korean life, from education to art and architecture.

One of the most iconic symbols of Korean traditional culture is the **hanbok**, a beautiful, intricate garment worn on special occasions. The hanbok is a testament to Korean craftsmanship, with its vibrant colors and delicate patterns. Visitors to Korea can experience the beauty of hanbok by attending a traditional tea ceremony or by trying on a hanbok at a cultural center.

The **royal palaces** of Korea are another must-visit destination for anyone interested in Korean traditional culture. The Gyeongbokgung Palace, built in 1395, is the largest and most iconic palace in Korea, showcasing the country's architectural prowess. The Changdeokgung Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is another notable palace that offers a glimpse into Korea's rich history.

Ancestral rites, known as **jesa**, are an essential part of Korean traditional culture. These rites honor the ancestors and the deceased, and are typically performed during the **Chuseok** and **Seollal** holidays. Visitors can experience these rituals by attending a traditional ceremony or by visiting a **sajikdan**, a shrine dedicated to the ancestors.

For those interested in learning more about Korean traditional culture, the **National Museum of Korea** is an excellent resource. The museum offers a wide range of exhibits and interactive displays that showcase Korea's history, art, and culture. The **Korean Folk Village** is another great destination, where visitors can experience traditional Korean life and crafts.

To delve deeper into Korean traditional culture, consider watching **'Dong Yi'**, a historical drama that explores the life of a royal concubine during the Joseon dynasty. For a more immersive experience, try **'hanbok rental'** services, which allow visitors to wear traditional Korean clothing and take photos in scenic locations. Finally, check out **'The Koreans: Their History and Culture'** by Kyoung-Min Han, a comprehensive book that explores Korea's history and cultural heritage.
