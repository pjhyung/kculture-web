Korean traditional culture is a treasure trove of history, philosophy, and aesthetics. At the heart of this rich heritage lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled Korea from 1392 to 1910. During this period, the Koreans developed a unique and distinctive culture that still influences the country today.

One of the most iconic symbols of Korean traditional culture is the **hanbok**, a beautifully designed traditional garment that reflects the country's history and social hierarchy. The hanbok is characterized by its vibrant colors, intricate patterns, and elegant silhouettes. Visitors to Korea can experience the beauty of hanbok by trying one on at a traditional tea house or by attending a hanbok fashion show.

Korea's royal palaces are another must-visit destination for anyone interested in traditional culture. The **Gyeongbokgung Palace**, built in 1395, is the largest and most iconic palace in Korea, showcasing the country's architectural prowess and historical significance. The **Changdeokgung Palace**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is another notable palace that offers a glimpse into the lives of Korean royalty.

Ancestral rites, known as **jesa**, are an essential part of Korean traditional culture. These rituals are performed to honor one's ancestors and are typically held during special occasions such as the **Seollal** (Lunar New Year) and **Chuseok** (Harvest Festival) holidays. Visitors can learn about these rituals by attending a traditional Korean ceremony or by visiting a museum that showcases Korean folk culture.

For those interested in exploring Korean traditional culture, there are several resources available. The **Korean Folk Village** in Yongin, Gyeonggi Province, is a living museum that showcases traditional Korean houses, farms, and artisans. The **National Museum of Korea** in Seoul is another excellent resource, featuring a vast collection of Korean artifacts and exhibits.

To experience the best of Korean traditional culture, consider watching **'Crash Landing on You'**, a popular K-drama that showcases Korean culture and scenery. You can also try **'Kong: Skull Island'**, a movie that features Korean landscapes and traditional clothing. For a unique cultural experience, visit the **Insa-dong** district in Seoul, known for its traditional tea houses, street performers, and Korean handicrafts.
