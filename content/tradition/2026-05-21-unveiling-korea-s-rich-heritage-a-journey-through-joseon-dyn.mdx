Korea's traditional culture is a treasure trove of history, philosophy, and art. At the heart of this rich heritage lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled the country from 1392 to 1910. During this period, Korean culture flourished, and many customs and traditions that are still celebrated today were born.

One of the most iconic symbols of Korean traditional culture is the **hanbok**, a beautifully designed garment that has been worn for centuries on special occasions such as weddings, holidays, and ancestral rites. The hanbok is a testament to Korean craftsmanship and attention to detail, with intricate designs and colors that reflect the wearer's social status and occasion.

Korea's royal palaces, such as **Gyeongbokgung** and **Changdeokgung**, are another window into the country's rich past. These stunning structures showcase the architectural genius and aesthetic sensibilities of the Joseon dynasty, with their harmonious blend of natural surroundings and man-made beauty.

Ancestral rites, known as **Jesa**, are an essential part of Korean traditional culture, where families pay respects to their ancestors through rituals and offerings. This practice is rooted in Confucianism, which emphasizes the importance of filial piety and respect for one's elders.

For foreigners interested in experiencing Korean traditional culture, there are many resources available. The **Korean Cultural Heritage Administration** offers a wealth of information on Korea's cultural heritage, including historical sites, festivals, and traditional crafts. Visitors can also explore the **National Museum of Korea**, which houses a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits on Korean history and culture.

To delve deeper into Korean traditional culture, consider watching **'Crash Landing on You'**, a popular K-drama that showcases Korea's rich cultural heritage, or try **'Hanbok try-on experience'** at a traditional Korean clothing store. You can also visit **Bukchon Hanok Village**, a charming traditional village in Seoul that offers a glimpse into Korea's past.
