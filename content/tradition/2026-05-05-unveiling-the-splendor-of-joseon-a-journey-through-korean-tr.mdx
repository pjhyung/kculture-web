Korean traditional culture is a treasure trove of vibrant customs, stunning architecture, and profound philosophies. At the heart of this rich heritage lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled Korea from 1392 to 1910. During this period, the Korean people developed a unique and distinctive culture that continues to inspire and fascinate people around the world.

One of the most iconic symbols of Korean traditional culture is the hanbok, a beautifully crafted traditional garment that is still worn on special occasions today. The hanbok is characterized by its elegant lines, vibrant colors, and intricate designs, which reflect the wearer's social status, occupation, and marital status. Visitors to Korea can experience the beauty of hanbok firsthand by trying one on at a traditional tea house or cultural center.

Korea's royal palaces are another must-see destination for anyone interested in traditional culture. The Gyeongbokgung Palace, built in 1395, is the largest and most iconic of these palaces, featuring an impressive array of traditional Korean architecture, gardens, and artifacts. The Changdeokgung Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is another notable example, showcasing the exquisite beauty of Korean palace design.

Ancestral rites are an essential part of Korean traditional culture, with many Koreans still observing these customs today. The Jesa ceremony, for example, is a ritual in which families pay respects to their ancestors through offerings of food, drink, and incense. Visitors can learn more about these customs at the Korean Folk Village or the National Museum of Korea.

For those interested in delving deeper into Korean traditional culture, there are many resources available. The **Korean Cultural Heritage Administration** website offers a wealth of information on Korea's cultural treasures, including the **Gyeongbokgung Palace** and the **National Museum of Korea**. The **K-Fashion** brand also offers a range of beautiful hanbok-inspired clothing and accessories.
