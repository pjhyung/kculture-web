Korean traditional culture is a treasure trove of history, philosophy, and artistry. At its core lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled Korea from 1392 to 1910. This period is often referred to as the 'golden age' of Korean culture, marked by significant advancements in literature, music, and visual arts. 

One of the most iconic symbols of Korean traditional culture is the **hanbok**, a beautifully designed garment worn on special occasions such as weddings, festivals, and ancestral rites. The hanbok is characterized by its vibrant colors, intricate patterns, and elegant silhouette. Visitors to Korea can experience the wonder of hanbok by attending a traditional festival or by trying one on at a Hanbok rental shop in places like **Gyeongbokgung Palace** or **Bukchon Hanok Village**.

Korea's royal palaces are another testament to the country's rich cultural heritage. **Gyeongbokgung Palace**, the largest and most iconic palace, is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Korean history. The palace complex is home to the **National Folk Museum of Korea**, which showcases the country's traditional way of life. Other notable palaces include **Changdeokgung Palace** and **Deoksugung Palace**, each with its unique architecture and historical significance.

Ancestral rites, known as **Jesa**, play a vital role in Korean traditional culture. These rituals are performed to honor one's ancestors and are typically held during special occasions such as **Seollal** (Lunar New Year) and **Chuseok** (Harvest Festival). Visitors can learn more about ancestral rites by attending a traditional **Jesa** ceremony or by visiting the **Korean Folk Village** in Yongin.
