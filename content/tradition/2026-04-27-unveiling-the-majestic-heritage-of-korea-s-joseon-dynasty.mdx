Korea's traditional culture is a treasure trove of rich history, vibrant customs, and breathtaking architecture. The Joseon dynasty, which ruled from 1392 to 1910, played a significant role in shaping the country's cultural identity. During this period, the traditional Korean garment, **hanbok**, was born. Characterized by its elegant design and intricate patterns, hanbok is still worn today on special occasions, such as weddings and festivals.

One of the most iconic symbols of the Joseon dynasty is the **Gyeongbokgung Palace**, the largest and most majestic of the Five Grand Palaces. Built in 1395, this stunning palace showcases the dynasty's architectural prowess and is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Korean history. Another notable palace is the **Changdeokgung Palace**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a prime example of traditional Korean architecture.

Ancestral rites, known as **jesa**, are an essential part of Korean traditional culture. These rites are performed to honor one's ancestors and are typically conducted during the **Chuseok** harvest festival. Families gather to pay their respects, offer food and drink, and share stories about their ancestors.

For those interested in delving deeper into Korean culture, there are several resources available. The **Korean Cultural Heritage Administration** offers guided tours of the royal palaces, while the **National Museum of Korea** provides a wealth of information on the country's history and artifacts. To experience the beauty of hanbok, visit the **Bukchon Hanok Village**, a traditional Korean village filled with hanok (traditional Korean houses) and hanbok shops.

To further explore Korean culture, consider the following recommendations:
  * Watch the Korean drama **'Dong Yi'**, which tells the story of a young woman who becomes a royal consort during the Joseon dynasty.
  * Read **'The Memoirs of Lady Hyegyong'**, a historical novel that provides a unique glimpse into the life of a royal court lady during the 18th century.
  * Try **'hanji'**, a traditional Korean paper craft that is used to create beautiful and intricate designs.
