Korea's traditional culture is a treasure trove of vibrant customs, stunning architecture, and profound history. At the heart of this heritage lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled the peninsula from 1392 to 1910. During this period, the Joseon people developed a unique and distinctive culture that continues to captivate audiences worldwide.

One of the most iconic symbols of Korean traditional culture is the **hanbok**, a beautiful, intricately designed garment that has been worn for centuries. The hanbok is not only a testament to Korean craftsmanship but also a reflection of the country's social hierarchy and cultural values. Visitors to Korea can experience the beauty of hanbok firsthand by attending a traditional tea ceremony or by trying on a hanbok at a rental shop.

Korea's royal palaces are another must-see destination for anyone interested in traditional culture. The **Gyeongbokgung Palace**, built in 1395, is the largest and most iconic of the five palaces, featuring an impressive array of traditional Korean architecture, including the majestic **Geunjeongjeon Hall**. The **Changdeokgung Palace**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is another notable example, showcasing the beauty of Korean garden design and architecture.

Ancestral rites, known as **Jesa**, play a significant role in Korean traditional culture. These rites are performed to honor one's ancestors and to express gratitude for their contributions to the family. Visitors can learn more about Jesa and other traditional Korean customs by attending a cultural festival or by visiting the **National Museum of Korea**.

For those interested in exploring Korea's traditional culture, several resources are available. The **Korean Cultural Heritage Administration** offers a wealth of information on Korean history, culture, and traditions. The **Visit Korea** website provides travel guides, recommendations, and insider tips for exploring the country. Additionally, the **Korea Tourism Organization** offers a range of cultural experiences, including traditional craft workshops and cultural performances.

To delve deeper into Korean culture, consider watching **'The Throne' (2015)**, a historical drama film that explores the life of King Yeongjo, or reading **'The Memoirs of Lady Hyegyong'**, a historical novel that provides a unique glimpse into the life of a royal court lady during the Joseon dynasty. For a more immersive experience, try **Ottchil**, a traditional Korean lacquerware product that showcases the country's exquisite craftsmanship.
