Korea's traditional culture is a treasure trove of history, philosophy, and art, with the Joseon dynasty (1392-1910) being one of the most significant periods in the country's development. During this time, the kingdom flourished, and its culture became characterized by strict social hierarchies, Confucianism, and a vibrant arts scene.

At the heart of Korean traditional culture is the **hanbok**, a beautifully designed garment that has been worn for centuries. The hanbok is more than just a piece of clothing; it's a symbol of Korean identity and a reflection of the country's rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Korea can experience the beauty of the hanbok by attending a traditional tea ceremony or by renting a hanbok and exploring the city's historic sites.

The Joseon dynasty was also marked by the construction of magnificent **royal palaces**, such as Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung, which are now UNESCO World Heritage Sites. These palaces are a testament to the architectural genius and craftsmanship of the time, with intricate designs, vibrant colors, and stunning gardens. Visitors can take a guided tour of the palaces and learn about the history and significance of each site.

Ancestral rites, known as **jesa**, are an essential part of Korean traditional culture. These rituals are performed to honor one's ancestors and to show respect for the deceased. Visitors can learn about the significance of jesa by attending a traditional Korean festival, such as the **Seollal** (Lunar New Year) celebrations, which take place in January or February.

For those interested in learning more about Korean traditional culture, we recommend visiting the **National Museum of Korea**, which offers a comprehensive overview of the country's history and art. We also suggest trying **Ottogi** traditional Korean tea, which is made from high-quality ingredients and is perfect for sipping while exploring the city's historic sites. Additionally, watching **'Crash Landing on You'**, a popular Korean drama that showcases the country's traditional culture, is a great way to get a glimpse into the lives of Koreans during the Joseon dynasty.
