Korean traditional culture is a treasure trove of rich history, vibrant customs, and breathtaking architecture. At the heart of this cultural landscape lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled Korea from 1392 to 1910. During this period, the Joseon royal family developed a unique and intricate system of etiquette, rituals, and customs that continue to fascinate people around the world.

One of the most iconic symbols of Korean traditional culture is the **hanbok**, a beautifully crafted traditional garment that reflects the wearer's social status, occupation, and marital status. The hanbok is characterized by its vibrant colors, intricate embroidery, and elegant silhouettes. Visitors to Korea can experience the beauty of hanbok by renting and wearing one at traditional tea houses or cultural festivals.

The **royal palaces** of the Joseon dynasty are another must-visit destination for anyone interested in Korean traditional culture. The most famous palace, **Gyeongbokgung**, is a stunning example of Korean architecture, with its majestic halls, serene gardens, and imposing stone walls. Visitors can explore the palace grounds, watch the changing of the guard ceremony, and learn about the history of the Joseon royal family.

Ancestral rites, known as **jesa**, are an essential part of Korean traditional culture. These rituals honor the ancestors and reinforce family bonds, and are typically performed during special occasions such as Lunar New Year and Chuseok (Korean Thanksgiving). Visitors can learn about jesa by attending cultural festivals or visiting traditional Korean homes.

To delve deeper into Korean traditional culture, we recommend:
  * Visiting the **National Museum of Korea** to learn about the history and artifacts of the Joseon dynasty
  * Watching **'Crash Landing on You'**, a popular K-drama that showcases Korean culture and tradition
  * Trying **Ottogi** traditional Korean tea, which is made from high-quality green tea leaves and offers a unique taste experience
