Korean traditional culture is a treasure trove of vibrant history, stunning architecture, and meaningful customs. At the heart of this rich heritage lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled Korea from 1392 to 1910. During this period, the iconic hanbok, a traditional Korean garment, was born. The hanbok is characterized by its elegant lines, vibrant colors, and intricate designs, making it a staple of Korean cultural identity.

One of the most breathtaking aspects of Joseon culture is its royal palaces. The Gyeongbokgung Palace, built in 1395, is the largest and most iconic of the five remaining palaces in Seoul. This majestic palace showcases the grandeur of Joseon architecture, with its soaring roofs, ornate facades, and serene gardens. Visitors can explore the palace grounds, attend a traditional Korean tea ceremony, or watch the changing of the guard ceremony.

Ancestral rites are an integral part of Korean traditional culture. The Jesa ceremony, a ritual honoring one's ancestors, is still practiced today. This meaningful custom involves the preparation of traditional foods, such as tteok (rice cakes) and makgeolli (traditional rice wine), and the offering of respects to one's ancestors. For a deeper understanding of this custom, visitors can attend a Jesa ceremony at the Korea House, a cultural center in Seoul.

To experience the beauty of Korean traditional culture, visitors can explore the following recommendations:
* Watch the movie 'The Handmaiden' (2016), a psychological thriller set in 1930s Korea, which showcases the stunning hanbok and the intricate customs of the Joseon era.
* Visit the National Museum of Korea, which houses an extensive collection of Korean artifacts, including hanbok, ceramics, and calligraphy.
* Try the traditional Korean tea, Omija Cha, at the O'Sulloc Tea House in Seoul, which offers a serene atmosphere and a chance to experience Korean tea culture.
