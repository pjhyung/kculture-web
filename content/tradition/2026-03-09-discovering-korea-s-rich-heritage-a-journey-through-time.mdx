Korea's traditional culture is a treasure trove of rich history, vibrant customs, and breathtaking architecture. The Joseon dynasty, which ruled from 1392 to 1910, played a significant role in shaping the country's heritage. During this period, the **hanbok**, a traditional Korean garment, became an integral part of everyday life. Characterized by its elegant design and vibrant colors, the hanbok is still worn today on special occasions, such as weddings and festivals.

One of the most impressive aspects of Korean traditional culture is its stunning royal palaces. **Gyeongbokgung Palace**, the largest and most iconic palace in Korea, is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history and architecture. Built in 1395, the palace showcases the grandeur and majesty of the Joseon dynasty. Another notable palace is **Changdeokgung Palace**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, which is famous for its beautiful gardens and traditional Korean architecture.

Ancestral rites, known as **jesa**, are an essential part of Korean traditional culture. These rites are performed to honor and pay respects to one's ancestors, and are usually conducted during special occasions, such as the **Seollal** (Lunar New Year) and **Chuseok** (Harvest Festival) holidays. To experience these rites firsthand, visit the **National Folk Museum of Korea**, which offers insight into Korea's traditional culture and customs.

For those interested in learning more about Korean traditional culture, there are several resources available. The book **'Korean Folk Customs'** by the Korea Foundation provides an in-depth look at Korea's traditional customs and practices. The **Korean Cultural Heritage Administration** website is also an excellent resource, offering information on Korea's cultural heritage, including its history, architecture, and traditional arts.

To truly immerse yourself in Korean traditional culture, consider watching **'Crash Landing on You'**, a popular K-drama that showcases Korea's stunning landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. You can also try traditional Korean cuisine, such as **bibimbap** and **bulgogi**, at restaurants like **Tosokchon**, a famous restaurant in Seoul that serves delicious traditional Korean dishes.
