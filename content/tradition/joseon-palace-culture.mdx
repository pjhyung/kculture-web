The Joseon dynasty (1392–1897) built five grand palaces in Seoul, each serving a distinct ceremonial and administrative purpose. Gyeongbokgung (경복궁), the "Palace of Shining Happiness," was the primary royal residence and a masterpiece of Korean architectural harmony.

Palace life was governed by Confucian principles — a rigid hierarchy of king, court officials, royal family, and thousands of palace servants. The **Changdeokgung Secret Garden** (비원), a UNESCO World Heritage Site, exemplifies how Joseon royalty integrated nature into daily life.

The daily changing of the royal guard (수문장 교대의식) ceremony, still performed today at Gyeongbokgung, gives visitors a direct connection to this living history. Tickets are free, and the ceremony runs multiple times daily.
