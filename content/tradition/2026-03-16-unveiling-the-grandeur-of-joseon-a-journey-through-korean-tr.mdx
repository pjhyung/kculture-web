# Introduction to Korean Traditional Culture
Korean traditional culture is a treasure trove of rich history, vibrant customs, and breathtaking architecture. At the heart of this heritage lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled Korea from 1392 to 1910 and left an indelible mark on the country's identity. One of the most iconic symbols of Korean culture is the hanbok, a traditional garment that exudes elegance and sophistication.

# The Splendor of Royal Palaces
The Joseon dynasty was renowned for its magnificent royal palaces, which served as the epicenter of political power and cultural expression. Gyeongbokgung Palace, built in 1395, is the largest and most iconic of these palaces, boasting an impressive array of traditional Korean architecture and beautiful gardens. Visitors can explore the palace grounds, witness the changing of the guard ceremony, and marvel at the stunning vistas.

# Ancestral Rites and Traditions
Ancestral rites play a significant role in Korean traditional culture, with the Joseon dynasty placing great emphasis on honoring one's ancestors. The traditional Korean holiday of Chuseok, which falls on the 15th day of the 8th lunar month, is a time for families to come together and pay respects to their ancestors through rituals and offerings. To experience this tradition firsthand, visitors can attend a traditional Korean tea ceremony or participate in a guided tour of a Korean ancestral shrine.

# Exploring Korean Culture
For those interested in delving deeper into Korean traditional culture, there are numerous resources available. The Korean Cultural Heritage Administration offers a wealth of information on Korea's cultural treasures, including the [Korean Folk Village](https://www.koreanfolk.co.kr/) and the [National Museum of Korea](https://www.museum.go.kr/). Visitors can also explore the vibrant streets of [Insa-dong](https://www.insadong.info/), a hub of traditional Korean culture and crafts.
