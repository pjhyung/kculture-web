Korean traditional culture is a treasure trove of vibrant customs, stunning architecture, and profound philosophies. At the heart of this heritage lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled Korea from 1392 to 1910. During this period, the Joseon kingdom developed a unique and distinctive culture that continues to influence Korean society today.

One of the most iconic symbols of Korean traditional culture is the **hanbok**, a beautifully crafted garment worn on special occasions such as weddings, festivals, and ancestral rites. The hanbok is characterized by its elegant lines, vibrant colors, and intricate designs, reflecting the social status, occupation, and marital status of the wearer.

For a glimpse into the grandeur of the Joseon dynasty, visitors can explore the majestic **royal palaces** of Korea, including Gyeongbokgung, Changdeokgung, and Bukchon Hanok Village. These architectural marvels showcase the advanced engineering skills, artistic flair, and philosophical values of the Joseon people.

Ancestral rites, known as **jesa**, play a significant role in Korean traditional culture, where families pay respects to their ancestors through elaborate ceremonies and offerings. This practice is rooted in Confucianism, which emphasizes the importance of filial piety, social hierarchy, and communal harmony.

To experience the beauty and richness of Korean traditional culture, visitors can attend a **Korean traditional music performance** at the National Gugak Center or watch a **Korean traditional dance** at the Korea House. For a deeper understanding of the Joseon dynasty, the **National Museum of Korea** offers a comprehensive exhibit on Korean history and culture.

For those interested in trying on a hanbok, **Konggan** in Insadong offers a wide range of traditional garments for rent. Meanwhile, **Tteok Museum** in Seoul provides a fascinating insight into the art of Korean traditional sweets and desserts. Lastly, **Sura** restaurant in Seoul serves an exquisite range of Korean traditional cuisine, including royal court dishes from the Joseon dynasty.
