Korean traditional culture is a treasure trove of vibrant history, breathtaking architecture, and exquisite artistry. At the heart of this cultural landscape lies the Joseon dynasty, which ruled Korea from 1392 to 1910. During this period, the Joseon people developed a unique and refined cultural identity, characterized by the stunning beauty of hanbok, the traditional Korean garment.

One of the most iconic symbols of Korean traditional culture is the hanbok, which has been a staple of Korean fashion for centuries. The hanbok is a testament to the country's rich textile heritage, with intricate designs, vibrant colors, and exquisite embroidery. Visitors to Korea can experience the beauty of hanbok firsthand by attending a traditional Korean tea ceremony or by trying on a hanbok at a local rental shop.

Korea's royal palaces are another must-see destination for anyone interested in Korean traditional culture. The Gyeongbokgung Palace, built in 1395, is the largest and most iconic of the five palaces, showcasing the grandeur and majesty of the Joseon dynasty. Visitors can explore the palace's impressive architecture, beautiful gardens, and historic artifacts, including the famous changing of the guard ceremony.

Ancestral rites are an integral part of Korean traditional culture, with the Joseon people placing great importance on honoring their ancestors. The Jesa ceremony, a traditional Korean ancestral ritual, is a significant part of Korean culture, where families pay respects to their ancestors through food, drink, and other offerings. Visitors can learn more about ancestral rites by visiting the Korean Folk Village or attending a traditional Korean festival.

For those interested in experiencing Korean traditional culture, there are many resources available. The **Korean Cultural Heritage Administration** offers guided tours of the royal palaces, while the **National Museum of Korea** provides a wealth of information on Korean history and culture. Visitors can also try on a hanbok at **Hanboknam**, a popular hanbok rental shop in Seoul.
