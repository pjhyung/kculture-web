Korean traditional culture is a treasure trove of history, philosophy, and aestheticism. The Joseon dynasty, which ruled from 1392 to 1910, laid the foundation for many of the customs and practices that are still observed today. One of the most distinctive aspects of Korean traditional culture is the **hanbok**, a beautifully crafted garment that has been worn for centuries on special occasions.

The **hanbok** is a testament to the ingenuity and craftsmanship of Korean artisans, with its intricate designs, vibrant colors, and elegant silhouettes. Visitors to Korea can experience the splendor of the **hanbok** firsthand by attending a traditional tea ceremony or by visiting one of the many **royal palaces** that dot the landscape. Gyeongbokgung Palace, the largest and most iconic of these palaces, is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Korean history and culture.

In addition to its stunning architecture and beautiful gardens, Gyeongbokgung Palace is also a hub for cultural activities and events. Visitors can watch traditional performances, such as the changing of the guard ceremony, or take part in **ancestral rites**, which are an integral part of Korean traditional culture. These rites, which involve the veneration of ancestors and the honoring of one's heritage, are a unique and fascinating aspect of Korean culture.

For those interested in delving deeper into Korean traditional culture, there are many excellent resources available. The **Korean Cultural Heritage Administration** offers a wealth of information on Korean history, culture, and traditions. Additionally, visitors to Korea can purchase traditional Korean products, such as **hanji** (Korean paper) and **noren** (Korean fabric), at markets and shops throughout the country.

Recommended viewing includes the film **'The Handmaiden'**, which offers a glimpse into the lives of women in the Joseon dynasty. For a more immersive experience, visitors can stay at a traditional **hanok** (Korean house) in the city of Bukchon, which is famous for its well-preserved traditional architecture.
