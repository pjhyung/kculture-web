Korean mythology is a treasure trove of fascinating creatures and tales that have been passed down for generations. At the heart of Korean folklore lie the dokkaebi, mischievous goblins known for their love of playing tricks on humans. These goblins are often depicted as short, hairy creatures with a penchant for music and dance. According to legend, dokkaebi possess supernatural strength and can be either benevolent or malevolent, depending on how they are treated by humans.

Another iconic creature in Korean mythology is the gumiho, a nine-tailed fox with magical powers. With its origins in Chinese mythology, the gumiho has evolved into a uniquely Korean creature, often depicted as a beautiful woman with seductive powers. In Korean folklore, the gumiho is said to possess the ability to shape-shift and grant wishes to those who treat it with kindness.

For those interested in delving deeper into Korean mythology, there are several resources available. The book *Korean Folk Tales* by Zong In-Sob provides a comprehensive collection of traditional Korean stories, including those featuring the dokkaebi and gumiho. The *National Folk Museum of Korea* in Seoul is also a must-visit, with its extensive exhibits on Korean folk culture and mythology.

To experience the magic of Korean mythology firsthand, consider watching the popular Korean drama *My Love From the Star*, which features a gumiho as one of its main characters. Alternatively, try playing the popular Korean video game *MapleStory*, which features dokkaebi as one of its many mythical creatures.

In addition to these recommendations, visitors to Korea can also explore the *Bukchon Hanok Village* in Seoul, which offers a glimpse into traditional Korean culture and architecture. With its rich history and vibrant culture, Korean mythology has something to offer everyone, from the curious traveler to the avid fan of fantasy and folklore.
