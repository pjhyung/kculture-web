Korean mythology is a rich and fascinating realm, filled with mystical creatures that have captivated the imagination of people for centuries. At the heart of this mythology are two iconic creatures: the dokkaebi goblins and the gumiho nine-tailed fox.

The dokkaebi are mischievous goblins known for their love of play and trickery. According to legend, they possess supernatural powers and are often depicted as carrying a magical club called a 'dokkaebi bangmangi'. These goblins are said to inhabit the mountains and forests of Korea, where they play tricks on unsuspecting travelers.

In contrast, the gumiho is a more sinister creature. With its nine tails and ability to shape-shift, this fox spirit is feared and respected in equal measure. According to legend, the gumiho has the power to seduce and manipulate humans, and is often depicted as a beautiful woman.

Korean folklore is full of stories about these creatures, and they continue to inspire and influence Korean culture to this day. For example, the popular Korean drama 'My Love From the Star' features a gumiho character, while the film 'The Tale of the Dokkaebi' brings the mischievous goblin to life on the big screen.

For those interested in learning more about Korean mythology, there are many resources available. The book 'Korean Folklore' by Zong In-Sob is a great place to start, as it provides a comprehensive introduction to the subject. The [National Folk Museum of Korea](https://www.nfm.go.kr/en/) in Seoul is also worth visiting, as it features exhibits on Korean folklore and mythology. Additionally, the K-drama 'Gumiho: Tale of the Fox's Child' is a great watch for those who want to see these creatures come to life.

In terms of products, the [Korean Folk Village](https://www.koreanfolk.co.kr/) in Yongin is a great place to buy traditional Korean crafts and souvenirs, including those featuring dokkaebi and gumiho characters. The [Kakao Friends] store also features a range of cute and quirky products featuring Korean folklore characters.
