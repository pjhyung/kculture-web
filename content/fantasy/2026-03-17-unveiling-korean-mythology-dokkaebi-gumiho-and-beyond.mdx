Korean mythology is a rich and fascinating realm, filled with magical creatures, supernatural beings, and timeless tales. At the heart of this mythology are the dokkaebi, mischievous goblins known for their love of mischief and mayhem. These goblins are often depicted as short, grotesque creatures with a penchant for playing tricks on humans.

Another iconic figure in Korean mythology is the gumiho, a nine-tailed fox with the power to shape-shift and seduce. According to legend, the gumiho possesses great beauty and cunning, using its charms to lure men to their doom. This mythical creature has been featured in numerous Korean folklore tales, including the classic novel *The Tale of the Nine-Tailed Fox*.

Korean folklore is also home to a wide range of other supernatural beings, including the dragon-like creature known as the Yong, and the heavenly maidens known as the Cheonnyeon. These creatures are often featured in traditional Korean stories, such as *The Myth of the Yongwang* and *The Legend of the Cheonnyeon*.

For those interested in exploring Korean mythology further, there are several great resources available. The *Korean Folk Village* in Yongin, Gyeonggi Province, is a living museum that showcases traditional Korean culture and folklore. Visitors can explore replica villages, watch traditional performances, and even try their hand at making traditional crafts.

In addition to visiting the Korean Folk Village, readers may also enjoy watching the Korean drama *My Girl* (, , 2005), which features a gumiho as one of its main characters. For a more in-depth look at Korean mythology, the book *Korean Folk Tales* by Zong In-Sob is a great resource, featuring a wide range of traditional stories and legends.

Whether you're interested in dokkaebi, gumiho, or other aspects of Korean mythology, there's no denying the rich cultural heritage of this fascinating country. So why not delve into the world of Korean folklore and discover the magic for yourself?
