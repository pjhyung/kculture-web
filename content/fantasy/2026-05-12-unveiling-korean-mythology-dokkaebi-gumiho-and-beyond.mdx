Korean mythology is a rich and fascinating world of legends, creatures, and tales that have been passed down for generations. At the heart of this mythology are the dokkaebi, mischievous goblins known for their love of troublemaking and clever antics. These goblins are often depicted as short, horned creatures with a fondness for drinking and music.

Another iconic creature in Korean mythology is the gumiho, a nine-tailed fox with magical powers and a penchant for transformation. According to legend, the gumiho can take on many forms, from a beautiful woman to a powerful spirit. In some tales, the gumiho is portrayed as a seductress, using her charms to lure men to their doom.

Korean folklore is full of stories about these and other creatures, such as the dragon-like yong and the benevolent spirit, sansin. Many of these tales have been passed down through oral tradition, with stories being shared and retold through generations.

For those interested in learning more about Korean mythology, there are many resources available. The book *Korean Folk Tales* by Zong In-Sob is a great place to start, offering a collection of traditional stories and legends. The *National Folk Museum of Korea* in Seoul is also a must-visit, with exhibits and displays showcasing the country's rich cultural heritage.

In addition, the popular Korean drama *My Roommate is a Gumiho* offers a modern take on the traditional gumiho legend, and is a great way to experience Korean mythology in a more contemporary context. For a taste of traditional Korean culture, try the *O'Sulloc Tea House* in Seoul, which offers a range of teas and snacks in a beautifully designed setting.

Whether you're interested in history, culture, or simply great stories, Korean mythology has something to offer. With its rich cast of characters and fascinating legends, it's a world that's waiting to be explored and discovered.
