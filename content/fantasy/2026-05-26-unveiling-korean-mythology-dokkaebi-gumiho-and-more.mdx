Korean mythology is a fascinating realm of magical creatures, legendary beings, and supernatural events. At its core are the dokkaebi, mischievous goblins known for their love of liquor, music, and mayhem. These mythical creatures are often depicted as short, green, and grotesque, with a penchant for playing tricks on humans.

Another iconic figure in Korean mythology is the gumiho, a nine-tailed fox with the power to shape-shift and seduce. According to legend, the gumiho can take on various forms, from a beautiful woman to a powerful fox, and is often associated with good fortune and prosperity.

Korean folklore tales are filled with stories of these creatures, as well as other mythical beings like the dragon-like yong and the heavenly maidens known as the cheonnyeon. These tales have been passed down through generations, influencing Korean culture and inspiring countless works of art, literature, and film.

For those interested in exploring Korean mythology further, there are several recommendations. The novel *The Hen Who Dreamed She Could Fly* by Sun-mi Hwang is a great introduction to Korean folklore, while the film *My Girlfriend is a Gumiho* is a fun and lighthearted take on the gumiho legend. Visitors to Korea can also explore the National Folk Museum of Korea in Seoul, which features exhibits on Korean mythology and folklore.

In addition, the Korean drama *Goblin* is a popular and highly-acclaimed series that draws inspiration from Korean mythology. The show's storyline, which features a goblin and a high school student, is a unique blend of mythology and modern-day life.

Korean mythology is a rich and vibrant world, full of fascinating creatures and legendary beings. Whether through literature, film, or cultural events, there are many ways to experience and appreciate this unique aspect of Korean culture.
