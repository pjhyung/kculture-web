Korean mythology is a treasure trove of fascinating creatures and tales that have been passed down for generations. At the heart of Korean folklore are the dokkaebi, mischievous goblins known for their love of tricks and troublemaking. These mythical beings are often depicted as short, grotesque creatures with a fondness for liquor and music.

Another iconic figure in Korean mythology is the gumiho, a nine-tailed fox spirit with the power to shape-shift and seduce humans. According to legend, the gumiho can take on various forms, from a beautiful woman to a powerful fox, and is often associated with good fortune and prosperity.

Korean folklore tales are not just limited to these two creatures, however. The country's rich cultural heritage is filled with stories of magical beings, supernatural events, and legendary heroes. For example, the story of the *Hwangsanbeol*, a mythological figure who brought fire to humanity, is still widely told and retold today.

For those interested in delving deeper into Korean mythology, there are several resources available. The *Korean Folk Village* in Yongin, Gyeonggi Province, is a great place to learn about traditional Korean culture and folklore. Visitors can explore the village's many museums, performances, and exhibits, which showcase the country's rich cultural heritage.

To learn more about dokkaebi and gumiho, readers can pick up a copy of *Korean Folk Tales* by Zong In-Sob, a collection of traditional stories that have been passed down through the generations. Alternatively, they can watch the popular Korean drama *My Love From the Star*, which features a gumiho as one of its main characters.

In terms of products, those interested in Korean mythology can check out the *Dokkaebi* beer, a Korean craft beer that features the mischievous goblin on its label. For a more immersive experience, visitors can attend the *Boryeong Mud Festival*, which features a range of muddy activities and events, including a dokkaebi-themed mud slide.
