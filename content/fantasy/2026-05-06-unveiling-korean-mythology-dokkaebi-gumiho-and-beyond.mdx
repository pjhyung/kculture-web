# Introduction to Korean Mythology
Korean mythology is a rich and fascinating realm, filled with unique creatures and legendary tales. At the heart of this mythology are the dokkaebi, mischievous goblins known for their love of tricks and pranks. These goblins are often depicted as short, grotesque creatures with a penchant for playing tricks on humans.

# The Gumiho: Nine-Tailed Fox
Another prominent figure in Korean mythology is the gumiho, a nine-tailed fox spirit with magical powers. According to legend, the gumiho possesses the ability to transform into various forms, including a beautiful woman. With its cunning and magical abilities, the gumiho is a revered and feared creature in Korean folklore.

# Exploring Korean Folklore Tales
Korean folklore is replete with stories of these mythical creatures, as well as other legends and tales. For those interested in delving deeper into Korean mythology, there are numerous resources available. The Korean Folk Village in Yongin, Gyeonggi Province, is a great place to start, offering a glimpse into traditional Korean culture and folklore.

# Recommendations for Further Exploration
For a deeper understanding of Korean mythology, consider watching the Korean drama *My Love From the Star*, which features a gumiho as one of its main characters. Additionally, the book *Korean Folk Tales* by Zong In-Sob provides an excellent collection of traditional Korean stories. Visitors to Korea can also try the traditional rice wine, makgeolli, which is often associated with dokkaebi and other mythical creatures.
