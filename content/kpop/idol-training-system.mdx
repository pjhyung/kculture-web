The K-pop industry operates on a unique trainee system where aspiring artists sign with major entertainment companies (SM, HYBE, YG, JYP) and undergo years of rigorous training before debut. Training covers vocals, dance, foreign languages, and media presentation — often beginning as young as 12-13 years old.

Major companies host global auditions, accepting trainees from Korea, Japan, China, Thailand, and increasingly Western countries. The average trainee period lasts 2-4 years, though some train for over a decade before debuting.

The system produces technically polished performers but has faced criticism for its demanding nature. Recent years have seen increased attention to trainee welfare, with companies implementing mental health support programs.
