Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is a city that seamlessly blends traditional heritage with modern lifestyle. The MZ generation, comprising Millennials and Gen Z, is at the forefront of shaping the city's culture, driven by their passion for technology, social media, and self-expression. 

Korean beauty trends, known as K-Beauty, have gained worldwide recognition for their innovative products and ingredients. The concept of 'glass skin' – achieving a smooth, luminous complexion – is highly sought after, with popular products like **Innisfree's Green Tea Seed Serum** and **Dr. Jart+'s Dermaclar Clearing Solution** being staples in many Korean skincare routines.

Seoul's fashion districts, such as Myeong-dong, Gangnam, and Hongdae, are must-visit destinations for anyone interested in Korean fashion. These areas offer a wide range of styles, from high-end designer boutiques to independent vintage shops and streetwear stores. **Gangnam's Rodeo Street**, in particular, is famous for its upscale shopping experience, with brands like Gucci and Louis Vuitton having flagship stores there.

The city's food scene is also heavily influenced by the MZ generation, with a focus on Instagrammable cafes and restaurants serving unique, visually appealing dishes. **Café Onion** in Hongdae is a popular spot, known for its quirky interior and delicious desserts.

Seoul's modern lifestyle is a testament to the city's ability to evolve and adapt, while still maintaining its rich cultural heritage. Whether you're interested in beauty, fashion, food, or technology, Seoul has something to offer, making it a fascinating destination for foreigners to explore and experience.
