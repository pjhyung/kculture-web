Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is a city that seamlessly blends traditional and modern lifestyle. The MZ generation, which includes Millennials and Gen Z, plays a significant role in shaping the city's culture. They are known for their love of technology, social media, and Korean pop culture, also known as K-pop.

The MZ generation's influence can be seen in the city's beauty trends. Korean skincare and makeup have gained worldwide popularity, with many foreigners visiting Seoul to experience the latest beauty trends. The city is home to many cosmetics shops, including **Innisfree** and **Etude House**, which offer a wide range of skincare and makeup products. One of the most popular skincare trends in Seoul is the use of sheet masks, which are face masks made of a thin sheet of fabric soaked in serum.

Fashion is another aspect of Seoul's modern lifestyle that is heavily influenced by the MZ generation. The city has several fashion districts, including **Myeong-dong**, **Gangnam**, and **Hongdae**, which offer a wide range of shopping options, from high-end boutiques to independent fashion stores. These districts are also known for their street food, cafes, and nightlife, making them popular destinations for both locals and tourists.

For foreigners interested in Korean culture, Seoul has a lot to offer. The city has many museums, galleries, and cultural centers that showcase Korean art, history, and traditions. The **National Museum of Korea** is a must-visit attraction, with a collection of over 110,000 artifacts that tell the story of Korea's rich history.

To experience the best of Seoul's modern lifestyle, visitors can stay in the **Jongno** district, which is known for its upscale hotels, restaurants, and shopping centers. They can also visit the **Bukchon Hanok Village**, which is a traditional Korean village with many hanok (traditional Korean houses) that have been converted into cafes, shops, and restaurants.
