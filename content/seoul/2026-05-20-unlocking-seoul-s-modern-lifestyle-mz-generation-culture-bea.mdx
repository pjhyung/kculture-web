Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is a city that seamlessly blends traditional and modern lifestyles. The MZ generation, comprised of Millennials and Gen Z, is driving the city's contemporary culture. This generation is characterized by their love for technology, social media, and self-expression.

At the heart of Seoul's modern lifestyle is its thriving beauty industry. Korean beauty trends, also known as K-beauty, have gained worldwide recognition for their innovative products and routines. One of the most popular K-beauty trends is the 'glass skin' look, which emphasizes achieving a smooth, luminous complexion. To achieve this, many Koreans swear by products containing snail mucin, such as **Dr. Jart+ Dermaclar Clearing Solution**.

Seoul is also a shopper's paradise, with numerous fashion districts that cater to diverse styles and budgets. **Myeong-dong** is one of the most popular shopping destinations, offering a wide range of high-end and affordable fashion brands. Another notable district is **Hongdae**, known for its indie and vintage shops, as well as its street art scene. For a unique shopping experience, visitors can explore **Gwangjang Market**, which offers traditional Korean clothing, accessories, and souvenirs.

In addition to its beauty and fashion scenes, Seoul's modern lifestyle is also characterized by its vibrant food culture. The city is famous for its street food, with popular dishes like bibimbap, tteokbokki, and hotteok. For a truly immersive experience, visitors can try **Bukchon Hanok Village**, a traditional Korean village with many restaurants and cafes serving authentic Korean cuisine.

To stay connected and navigate the city, many foreigners rely on **T-Money**, a convenient prepaid card that can be used for public transportation, taxis, and even some shops and restaurants.
