Korean daily life is a unique blend of traditional and modern culture. From bustling streets to serene temples, Korea has something to offer for everyone. One of the best ways to experience Korean culture is through its street food. Popular dishes like **Tteokbokki** (spicy rice cakes) and **Hotteok** (sweet pancakes) can be found in almost every street market, with prices starting from as low as ₩1,000 (approximately $0.80 USD).

Convenience store culture is also a significant part of Korean daily life. Stores like **7-Eleven**, **CU**, and **GS25** are open 24/7, offering a wide range of products from snacks to toiletries. These stores often have a section dedicated to Korean snacks, such as **Pepero** (a popular chocolate-dipped cookie stick) and **Ramyeon** (Korean instant noodles).

For foreigners interested in Korean culture, learning a few basic Korean language expressions can go a long way. Phrases like **annyeonghaseyo** (hello), **gamsahamnida** (thank you), and **joheunhamnida** (excuse me) are essential for navigating daily life in Korea. The Korean government also offers various language courses and cultural programs for foreigners, such as the **Korean Language Course** at **Yonsei University**.

When visiting Korea, be sure to check out the **Myeong-dong** shopping district in Seoul, which offers a wide range of street food, shopping, and entertainment options. For a unique shopping experience, visit the **Gwangjang Market**, which is one of the oldest traditional markets in Korea.

To get a taste of Korean culture, try watching **Crash Landing on You**, a popular Korean drama that showcases the country's beautiful landscapes and rich culture. You can also listen to **K-pop** music, which has gained immense popularity worldwide, with groups like **BTS** and **Blackpink** leading the way.
