Korean daily life is a unique blend of traditional and modern practices. In Seoul, the capital city, you can find ancient temples and palaces alongside sleek skyscrapers and neon-lit streets. The Korean language, known as Hangul, is a key part of the culture, and foreigners can easily pick up basic expressions like 'annyeonghaseyo' (hello), 'gamsahamnida' (thank you), and 'joheunhamnida' (excuse me).

One of the best ways to experience Korean culture is through its street food. Popular dishes like tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), hotteok (sweet pancakes), and bungeo-ppang (fish-shaped pastry) can be found at street vendors and markets throughout the city. For a truly immersive experience, visit the Myeong-dong shopping district, where you can sample a variety of street foods while browsing the trendy shops and stalls.

Convenience store culture is also a big part of Korean daily life. Chains like 7-Eleven, CU, and GS25 offer a wide range of products, from snacks and drinks to cosmetics and clothing. Many Koreans visit convenience stores daily to grab a quick breakfast or snack, and some stores even offer services like bill payment and package delivery.

For foreigners interested in Korean culture, there are many resources available. The Korean government offers free language classes and cultural programs for foreigners, and many universities and language schools offer courses in Korean language and culture. You can also find many Korean language learning apps, such as Duolingo and Naver Dictionary, to help you get started.

Some popular Korean products that are worth trying include **Pepero** (a chocolate-dipped cookie stick), **Ramyeon** (a spicy instant noodle dish), and **KakaoTalk** (a popular messaging app). You can find these products at convenience stores or online at stores like **Gmarket** or **11th Street**.
