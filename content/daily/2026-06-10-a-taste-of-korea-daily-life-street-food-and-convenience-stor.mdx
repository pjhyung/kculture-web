Korean daily life is a unique blend of traditional and modern culture. From the bustling streets of Seoul to the quiet countryside, Korea offers a distinct experience for foreigners interested in its culture. 
One of the most notable aspects of Korean daily life is its street food culture. Popular dishes like *tteokbokki* (spicy rice cakes) and *hotteok* (sweet pancakes) can be found in almost every market and street corner. 
Convenience store culture is also thriving in Korea, with chains like 7-Eleven, CU, and GS25 offering a wide range of products, from food and drinks to cosmetics and household items. These stores are often open 24/7, making them a convenient option for locals and tourists alike.
To fully immerse oneself in Korean culture, learning a few basic Korean language expressions can go a long way. Phrases like *annyeonghaseyo* (hello), *gamsahamnida* (thank you), and *joheunhamnida* (excuse me) are essential for interacting with locals. 
For those interested in exploring Korean culture, some popular destinations include **Myeong-dong** for shopping and street food, **Gwangjang Market** for traditional Korean cuisine, and **Bukchon Hanok Village** for a glimpse into traditional Korean architecture.
Some must-try products include **Pepero** (a popular Korean snack), **Ramyeon** (Korean instant noodles), and **Kakao Friends** merchandise (based on the popular Korean messaging app).
