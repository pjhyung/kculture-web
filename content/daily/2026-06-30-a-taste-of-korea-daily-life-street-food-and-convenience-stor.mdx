Korean daily life is a unique blend of traditional and modern practices. In Seoul, the capital city, you can find ancient temples and palaces alongside modern skyscrapers and technology hubs. The Korean language, also known as Hangul, is a key part of the culture, with phrases like '*annyeonghaseyo*' (hello) and '*gamsahamnida*' (thank you) being essential for any visitor.

One of the best ways to experience Korean culture is through its street food. Popular dishes like '*tteokbokki*' (spicy rice cakes) and '*hotteok*' (sweet pancakes) can be found at street vendors and markets throughout the city. The *Myeongdong Night Market* in Seoul is a great place to try these dishes, as well as other Korean specialties like '*bibimbap*' (mixed rice bowl) and '*naengmyeon*' (cold noodles).

Convenience store culture is also a significant part of Korean daily life. Stores like *7-Eleven* and *CU* are open 24/7, offering a wide range of products, from snacks and drinks to cosmetics and electronics. Many Koreans also use these stores to pay bills, send packages, and even order food delivery. The *GS25* convenience store chain is particularly popular, with a wide selection of food and drinks, including the popular *Korea Yakult* probiotic drink.

For foreigners interested in Korean culture, there are many resources available to learn more. The *Korean Government's Official Website* offers a range of information on Korean culture, history, and language. The *Duolingo* language learning app also has a Korean course, which can help beginners learn the basics of the language.

In addition to trying the local street food and visiting convenience stores, there are many other ways to experience Korean culture. The *National Museum of Korea* is a great place to learn about Korean history and art, while the *Bukchon Hanok Village* offers a glimpse into traditional Korean architecture and lifestyle. The popular K-drama *Crash Landing on You* is also a great way to learn about Korean culture and language, with many scenes featuring traditional Korean food, clothing, and customs.
